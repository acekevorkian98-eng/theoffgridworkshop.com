Off-Grid Gear Reviews

Hands-on reviews of the products we’ve personally tested at our off-grid homestead. No paid placements. Affiliate links are always clearly marked.

Our Review Process

We test every product in real off-grid conditions — not ideal lab conditions. A solar panel that produces 95% of rated wattage in a field test is genuinely good. One that produces 80% is not, no matter what the spec sheet says. We also track long-term performance where possible.

Recommended

The most popular entry-level off-grid solar kit for good reason. Solid real-world performance, reasonable price, and available everywhere. The included 30A PWM charge controller is a weak point — upgrade to MPPT for better efficiency.

Jackery Explorer 1000 Pro Power Station

Good

A well-made portable power station ideal for camping, van life, and short-term off-grid use. Not designed as a permanent home backup — the 1002Wh capacity is limiting and Li-ion chemistry has fewer cycles than LiFePO4. For the use case it’s designed for, it’s excellent.

Water Systems

Big Berkey Gravity Water Filter

Recommended

The gold standard of gravity water filters. Removes 99.99% of bacteria, viruses, and a wide range of contaminants using no electricity. Expensive upfront but the filter elements last 3,000+ gallons each. For off-grid drinking water, this is the benchmark everything else is judged against.

LifeStraw Mission 12L Gravity Filter

Good

A more affordable gravity filter with hollow-fiber membrane technology. Excellent for emergency backup and camping. The 12L capacity is limiting for daily household use, but the value-to-performance ratio is outstanding.
