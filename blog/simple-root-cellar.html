Building a Simple Root Cellar: Our 18-Month Results

We built our root cellar for $1,200 and stored 400 pounds of produce. Here are the real storage results from 18 months — every vegetable, every month, with actual success rates.

By Jake Mercer · Off-Grid Homesteader & Former Engineer

Ventilation: What We Actually Do

The ventilation is what makes it work. Our routine:

Fall (October-November)

  • Open both vents at night when outside < 40°F
  • Close both during warm days
  • Check 2x daily — this is the transition

Winter (December-February)

  • Close cold-air inlet when outside < 20°F
  • Leave warm-air outlet cracked
  • Minimal management — cold does the work

Spring (March-April)

  • Manage vents daily as temps swing
  • Open at night, close when sun hits
  • Close completely by May

The $12 Thermometer

A min/max thermometer inside tells you the actual range over 24 hours — not just what it reads when you check. This is the most useful $12 we spent.

What We Would Do Differently

  • Divided rooms: Squash needs 50-60°F/60-70% humidity — different from root veg. We would build a partition.
  • Digital monitor: We check manually. A WiFi sensor would be easier.
  • Deeper floor: 4 inches is not enough drainage. Would go 6 inches.
  • More shelving: Floor storage takes more space than expected.
