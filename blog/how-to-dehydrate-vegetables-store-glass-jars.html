How to Dehydrate Vegetables: Our Tested Guide

We have dehydrated 400+ pounds of vegetables over 3 years. Here is the tested temperature guide, timing data, and our real shelf life results from glass jar storage — not theory, but what actually happens.

By Jake Mercer · Off-Grid Homesteader & Former Engineer

Our Tested Process

After 400+ lbs, here is what actually works:

Step 1: Prep

  • Wash thoroughly — no special wash needed
  • Slice consistent thickness (3-5mm) — inconsistent = uneven drying
  • Blanch when needed (carrots, corn, beans) — reduces drying time by ~30%

Step 2: Load

  • Single layer only — overlapping causes uneven drying
  • Leave gap between pieces for airflow
  • Rotate trays halfway (our dehydrator has hot spots)

Step 3: Dry

  • Our setting: 125°F (actual) for most vegetables
  • Check at manufacturer low end, not high end
  • The snap test: cool to room temp, bend — should snap, not bend

Step 4: Condition

  • Cool to room temp first (30 min)
  • Loose in a bowl, uncovered
  • Shake daily for 7 days
  • This equalizes moisture — prevents mold in jars

Skip Conditioning at Your Peril

We skipped this once (Year 1). Mold in 3 jars of carrots. Now we never skip the 7-day conditioning. It is not optional.

Step 5: Store

  • Wide-mouth quart jars
  • 300cc oxygen absorber per jar
  • Label with vegetable and date

Equipment We Use

After 3 years, here is what we recommend:

  • 9 trays, 26 sq ft total space
  • 125-165°F range
  • 600W — fits on our solar budget
  • Horizontal airflow = no tray rotation needed

Mandoline ($25)

  • Consistent 3-5mm slices
  • Uneven slices = uneven drying

Wide-Mouth Quart Jars ($1 each)

  • Easy packing and retrieval
  • Fits oxygen absorber
  • Standard mouth too tight

Common Mistakes We Made

  • Overlapping slices (Year 1): Mold in the middle of jar. Now single layer only.
  • Wrong temperature (Year 1): 155°F burned carrots. Now 125°F.
  • Not conditioning (Year 1): 3 jars of carrots went moldy. Now always 7 days.
  • Skipping blanch (Year 2): Carrots took 14 hours instead of 10. Now always blanch.
  • Not rotating (Year 2):Uneven drying. Now rotate trays at midpoint.
