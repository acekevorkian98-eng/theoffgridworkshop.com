Our Story

From urban life to sustainable homesteading

The Journey Begins

In 2015, we were living the typical urban lifestyle—commuting to office jobs, paying astronomical utility bills, and feeling increasingly disconnected from the natural world. Something was missing. We craved independence, self-sufficiency, and a deeper connection to the land.

That year, we made a decision that would change our lives forever. We left the city, purchased 20 acres of raw land in the Colorado mountains, and embarked on a journey to build our own off-grid homestead from the ground up.

Learning Through Experience

The first years were challenging. We made countless mistakes—from undersizing our solar array to accidentally contaminating our water tank. Every setback taught us valuable lessons that you won't find in textbooks. We experimented with different techniques, materials, and systems until we discovered what actually works in real-world conditions.

Through trial and error, we developed systems for:

  • A 5kW solar power system with battery backup that provides all our electricity needs
  • A rainwater harvesting system that captures and filters 8,000 gallons annually
  • A year-round greenhouse that produces 80% of our vegetables
  • A passive solar home design that requires minimal heating and cooling
  • Composting toilets and greywater systems for complete water independence

Sharing the Knowledge

As word spread about our homestead, friends and neighbors started asking questions. Could they do it too? How much does it cost? Where do you even start? We realized that our hard-won knowledge could help others avoid our mistakes and accelerate their own journeys to sustainability.

In 2018, we launched The OffGrid Workshop to share our practical, hands-on experience with anyone interested in sustainable living. We focus on real-world solutions, honest cost breakdowns, and step-by-step tutorials that anyone can follow—whether you're building a complete homestead or just want to reduce your environmental footprint.

Our Mission

We believe that sustainable living shouldn't be complicated, expensive, or exclusive. Our mission is to make off-grid knowledge accessible to everyone through clear, practical guides that demystify renewable energy, water systems, food production, and natural building techniques.

We're not selling a romanticized vision of homesteading—we're sharing the real triumphs and challenges, the actual costs and timeframes, and the genuine satisfaction of living in harmony with nature while maintaining modern comforts.
